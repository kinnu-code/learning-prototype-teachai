# Companies (Private Limited) Formation & Commencement

**Incorporation Process: Key Procedural Steps (CA 2006):**

| Step | Action | Authority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Prepare Application (<skim>Form IN01</skim>) | <skim>CA 2006 s.9</skim> | Includes proposed name, registered office, type (private/public), statement of capital, initial shareholdings, officers, <skim>PSC</skim>, statement of compliance |
| 2 | Submit to <skim>Companies House</skim> | CA 2006 s.9 | With fee, Articles (if bespoke), Memorandum (basic details) |
| 3 | <skim>Certificate of Incorporation</skim> issued | <skim>CA 2006 s.15</skim> | Confirms <skim>legal existence from this date</skim> |

**Constitutional Documents:**

- <skim>Articles of Association (AoA)</skim>: Main constitutional document (CA 2006)
  - <skim>Model Articles</skim> default unless bespoke articles adopted.
  - Amending AoA requires <skim>special resolution (≥75%)</skim> filed at Companies House within <skim>15 days</skim> (CA 2006 s.21, s.26).
  - <skim>Memorandum of Association</skim>: Defines initial subscribers and shareholdings; <skim>cannot be amended post-incorporation</skim> (CA 2006 s.8).
  - <skim>Entrenched articles</skim>: Require <skim>unanimous consent</skim> or special conditions to amend; notify Companies House (Forms CC01/CC02/CC03).

**Companies House Filing Requirements:**

- Annual <skim>confirmation statement</skim> (PSC details, directors, registered office)
- Notify changes within <skim>14 days</skim> (directors, secretaries, PSC)
- File special resolutions within <skim>15 days</skim>

**Commencing Operations (Practical Considerations):**

- No legal requirement to open bank account, appoint auditors, or obtain insurance before trading (though practically advisable).
- Company can <skim>trade immediately upon incorporation</skim>.

# Partnerships (General & Limited)

**General Partnership Formation:**

- <skim>No formalities required</skim>; arises when two or more persons carry on business with a view to profit (<skim>Partnership Act 1890, s.1</skim>).
- <skim>Unlimited liability</skim> (jointly for contracts; jointly & severally for torts).
- Default Provisions (PA 1890) unless varied by Partnership Agreement:
  - Partners owe <skim>fiduciary duties</skim> (e.g., good faith, no secret profits) unless modified by agreement.
  - <skim>Equal share of profits/losses</skim> regardless of capital contribution.
  - All partners entitled (but not required) to manage.
  - Majority decisions; <skim>unanimity required for fundamental changes</skim> (nature of business, new partners, varying rights).
  - <skim>No partner expulsion</skim> without prior unanimous agreement.
  - <skim>Automatic dissolution</skim> if partner leaves, dies, bankrupt, or by notice.

**Limited Partnership (LP) Formation:**

- At least one <skim>General Partner (unlimited liability)</skim> and one <skim>Limited Partner (limited liability)</skim>.
- Must register with Companies House (<skim>LP5 form</skim>) before trading.
- Often used as <skim>investment vehicles</skim> (private fund LPs).

**Partnership Agreements: Key Clauses:**

- Profit/loss sharing definitions
- Management roles
- Decision-making procedures
- Expulsion provisions
- Dissolution events

# Limited Liability Partnerships (LLPs)

**Formation Of LLP:**

| Step | Action | Authority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | File <skim>Form LL IN01</skim> at Companies House | <skim>LLP Act 2000</skim> | Name, registered office, <skim>designated members (min. 2)</skim>, lawful business |
| 2 | Certificate of Incorporation issued | LLP Act 2000 | LLP gains <skim>separate legal personality</skim> from this date |

**LLP Agreement:** *Key Clauses (Default if no agreement)*

- <skim>Equal profit sharing</skim>, no remuneration for management
- All members may manage; ordinary decisions by majority
- <skim>Unanimity for fundamental changes</skim> (nature of business, assigning membership)
- <skim>No implied expulsion power</skim>; must be expressly included
- Duty not to compete; duty to account for benefits derived from LLP business
- <skim>Clawback</skim> of members' drawings if LLP becomes insolvent (<skim>Insolvency Act 1986 s.214A</skim>).

**Filing Requirements & Initial Compliance:**

- Maintain registered office in UK
- Minimum <skim>two designated members</skim> responsible for filings (annual accounts, confirmation statement, PSC register)
- Members individually register with HMRC for self-assessment (<skim>LLP not subject to Corporation Tax</skim>)

# Professional Conduct & Ethical Considerations (SRA Principles)

**Acting Honestly & With Integrity:**

- Solicitors must uphold honesty, integrity, and independence when advising clients on business formation.

**Risks Of Conflict Of Interest:**

- Cannot act if <skim>substantial risk of conflict</skim> exists (e.g., advising LLP and individual member with competing interests).
- <skim>Informed consent insufficient</skim> if solicitor cannot act in best interests of all parties.

**Client Care & Confidentiality:**

- Maintain <skim>strict confidentiality</skim>; clearly communicate risks and obligations.
- Provide clear, accurate advice on formation options, <skim>liability implications</skim>, and <skim>tax consequences</skim>.

# Practical Tips:

**Companies:**

- Remember <skim>special resolution (≥75%)</skim> required to amend articles or disapply pre-emption rights.
- <skim>PSC register compliance mandatory</skim>; failure to comply can lead to penalties.

**Partnerships:**

- Always recommend written <skim>Partnership Agreement</skim> to avoid default statutory provisions.
- Clarify profit-sharing arrangements explicitly.

**LLPs:**

- LLP does not exist until Companies House issues incorporation certificate; members <skim>personally liable for pre-incorporation contracts</skim>.
- LLP itself is <skim>tax-transparent</skim>; members taxed individually.

# Quick Comparison Table: Liability & Formation Requirements

| Entity | Liability | Formation Formalities | Legal Personality | Taxation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Company | Limited | Companies House (IN01); AoA | Yes | Corporation Tax (close companies subject to special rules). |
| General Partnership | Unlimited | None required | No | Individual (self-assessment) |
| Limited Partnership | GP: Unlimited LP: Limited | Companies House (LP5) | No | Individual (self-assessment) |
| LLP | Limited | Companies House (LL IN01) | Yes | Individual (self-assessment) |
